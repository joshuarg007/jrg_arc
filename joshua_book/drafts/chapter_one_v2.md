# Chapter One: The Fool's Step

---

## I.

The clock on the pawnshop wall said 3:47 when he walked in. It said 3:47 when he walked out at least twenty minutes later with eighty bucks for grandma's ring. It said 3:47 when XM radio loaded. Only later did he wonder how there was an hour's worth of ice on his windshield.

Marcus was in the passenger seat. Nine years old. Eyes that didn't belong on a nine-year-old.

"Time's wrong again."

"Yup."

The same song from yesterday came on. Third time this hour. He drove.

---

## II.

She hadn't slept in four days.

Maps covered the walls. Strings and pushpins connecting photographs of nothing—or what looked like nothing. She'd been seeing cracks since she was twelve. Nobody else saw them. First psychiatrist called it schizophrenia. The pills made everything gray, so she stopped.

The cracks came back. She started mapping.

Tonight the main fracture—Brown Palace to Colfax to Five Points—had gone from hair-thin to finger-wide. Six hours.

She grabbed her camera. Headed for the door.

The strings on her map were moving.

Not physically. In that other way. Four points. Four places pulling the whole city toward them.

She was standing in one.

The ceiling opened. Not cracked—opened. Light that wasn't light poured through. Her photographs aged to dust. Strings rotted. Pushpins rusted in real-time.

She made the hallway. The threshold held it back somehow.

A year of work, gone.

But now she knew. Four points. She was one.

---

## III.

The Emperor gripped the sink. His reflection didn't move when he did.

"I see you."

It smiled.

*We see you too.*

"Back."

*We're inside you. Behind your eyes. Between your ribs. We are what your family was made to hold.*

"BACK."

It flinched. Became just a reflection. Tired eyes. Hair going gray too fast.

Another day bought. Maybe two.

His father made it to forty-two before the containment cracked. Grandfather, fifty-six. Great-grandfather, fifty-one.

He was thirty-four.

On the table, a letter from someone who shouldn't know his address:

*The four who were broken will become one. The seal is failing. Instrument or obstacle—choose wisely.*

---

## IV.

She woke up in his arms. Couldn't remember falling asleep.

This kept happening. Whole evenings gone. She'd be cooking, then she'd be in bed, food cold on the counter.

"You drifted off."

Same words. Same smile.

She was always tired now. Deep tired. Like something draining her.

*That's crazy*, she thought. *You're just—*

"Come back to me."

The thought slipped away.

She loved him. She was sure. When she was with him, everything faded. The exhaustion. The sense she used to be more.

Somewhere far back in her head, something screamed.

---

## V.

His father's apartment smelled like smoke and something sweeter burning.

"Thought you were dead."

"Not yet."

"Pity."

Same holes in the drywall. Same stains. TV blaring some infomercial.

"Need to know about what you served. Before you fell."

His father's knuckles went white on the armrests.

"The Witnesses. Tower off Champa. They serve what's behind the veil. Think they can use it." Harsh laugh. "Nobody uses that thing."

"That's why you ran."

"Didn't run fast enough. Still in me. Not controlling—watching. Everything I do. Thirty years." His eyes went somewhere else for a second. Something looked through them. Then gone. "They made your prophecy. The four. You're not heroes. You're ingredients."

"For what?"

"Breaking the seal. Letting everything through at once." He slumped. "Don't trust the convergence. Someone designed it. Wasn't for your benefit."

---

## VI.

The tower was twelve stories from outside. The Priestess pressed thirteen in the elevator.

The doors opened on a library. Wood shelves to a ceiling she couldn't see. Candles. A woman in robes that didn't belong to this century.

"You're the Witnesses."

"I'm the Reader." Hungry smile. "You have sight. We've been hoping you'd find us."

A book on the table. Old. Handwritten in something she shouldn't understand but did.

*The Seal and the Membrane.*

"The veil was never meant to be a wall," the Reader said. "It was permeable once. Things passed through. Ideas. Dreams. Someone got scared. Built the seal. That's what's breaking now. The veil trying to remember what it was."

The Priestess looked at the diagrams. The membrane version looked alive. The seal version looked like a scar.

"The prophecy," the Reader said. "The four. You were designed. Bred. Generations of it. You're not a warning. You're a recipe."

She ran.

---

## VII.

The Emperor found him in a bar off Larimer.

"You're one of the four."

"So they tell me."

"There's a woman. Downing Street. The Empress. Something's feeding on her." He slid a photograph across. Surveillance shot. Woman in a window.

The Magician knew that silhouette.

Twenty years since he'd seen her. Fifteen since he'd stopped looking.

"The thing with her leaves at 2 AM every night. Goes to the tower. You'd have a few hours."

"To do what?"

"Free her. You can do more than you know. Your grandfather was training you for it."

"He died."

"Then finish yourself."

The Emperor left. The bartender wiped a glass, watching with wrong eyes.

"You're one of them," the Magician said.

"We're everywhere. Convergence is coming whether you want it or not." The bartender smiled. "Instrument or obstacle?"

The Magician stood.

"Tell your people I'm neither. I'm what comes for anyone who tries to use me as either."

He walked out.

Behind him: "We're counting on it."

---

## VIII.

She woke alone.

Relief hit before she could stop it. Then guilt. How could she be relieved? She loved him.

*Stop.*

The voice came from somewhere buried.

*This isn't love. This is what he made you feel. He's eating you.*

She closed her eyes. Reached into the locked place.

Something stirred.

*Make something. Remember.*

She thought about light. Warmth.

Her palm tingled.

A mote of light. Size of a firefly. Warm. Real.

Tears. After everything. Still there.

The door opened. He was home.

She closed her fist. The light vanished.

"Missed me?"

"Always."

But underneath—a coal burning.

---

## IX.

The building was three floors from outside.

The Magician counted seven going up.

Each landing wronger than the last. Shadows falling directions they shouldn't. His breath came out static instead of fog.

Seventh floor. Red door. Brass knob pulsing like a heartbeat.

He opened it.

The room was full of her.

Pieces. Fragments. Futures.

Her at thirty, laughing, kid on her hip. Forty, speaking to a crowd. Sixty, fierce and gray-haired.

Dying. As he watched, one dissolved. Her at twenty-five, paint-stained and happy—gone like sugar in water.

Someone eating them.

Center of the room. Her on something like a bed. Eyes closed. And crouched over her, feeding—his brother.

Not just his brother. Something behind him. Smoke. Shadow. Size of the room.

*You found us.*

"Get off her."

*She's ours. Your love was a beacon. Your protection was a map.*

He moved.

The thing moved his brother's body wrong. Angles that didn't exist. Speed that wasn't human.

His ribs cracked. He hit the wall. His brother stood over him.

*Last words?*

He looked at the room. Pocket carved out of the veil.

Pockets could collapse.

"This isn't a room. It's a wound."

He reached down. Into the space between thought and thing.

He pulled.

Everything screamed. The futures reversed. Time running backward through them.

Reality cleared its throat.

Third-floor landing. Normal walls.

His brother gone.

She was there. Against the wall. Looking at him like something remembered.

"We need to go."

"He'll come back."

"Then we don't let him find you."

Three blocks away, the Priestess was waiting.

"You collapsed it. Half the cracks in the city just moved."

She looked at the woman in his arms.

"She's the Empress."

"Don't know what that means."

"You will." The Priestess checked the air. "The Emperor's close. Containment's failing. We need to move."

---

## X.

Cheesman Park. 3 AM.

The Emperor stood in a circle of dead grass. Fifteen feet wide. Shaking at a frequency that made teeth hurt.

"Don't come closer."

The dead grass was spreading. At the edges, blades weren't just dying—they were unbecoming.

"He's leaking," the Priestess said.

The Emperor fell. The ring surged three feet.

The Magician walked in.

The wrongness hit like water. Thicker. Something with opinions. By the time he reached center, he'd forgotten his grandfather's face.

"Let me help."

"You can't. It's genetic."

The Magician grabbed his hand.

Everything surged.

---

## XI.

From outside the circle: contact made, dead grass stopped spreading. One beat. Two.

Then started contracting.

Two currents wrapping around each other. The Emperor's poison and the Magician's will.

"The Empress needs to be here," the Priestess said.

"I can't. Nothing left."

"Stolen things can be stolen back."

The Priestess took her hand. Reached past the damage. The manufactured love. The hollow places.

Found the seed.

*Wake up.*

Nothing.

*Tell her the truth*, something whispered. *She needs to remember why she stopped.*

The Priestess went deeper.

---

## XII.

Eight years old. Making a bird.

Not imagining. Making. The sparrow sat on her palm. Real because she made it real.

The door opened. Her brother walked in.

Not blood—their parents had been together two years. But she'd learned to call him that.

She didn't trust him.

"What's that?"

"Nothing."

"Give it."

"No."

He grabbed her wrist.

Something passed from the bird to him.

The sparrow collapsed. Not dead—unmade.

He smiled.

"You're going to make me so many things."

She stopped making things after that. Buried it. By the time he came for her as an adult, she'd forgotten she ever could.

It hadn't protected her.

---

## XIII.

The Priestess pulled back.

The Empress was crying. Remembering.

"I stopped because of him. Then he took me anyway."

"You were a child."

"I knew what he was."

She looked at the circle. At the two men holding something back. At the sky above them.

The sky was cracking.

The veil. Visible now. Fault lines through the stars.

"I can see it," the Empress said. Different voice. Stronger. "I can see what we're supposed to do."

Something looked down through the crack.

---

## XIV.

The Magician felt the fourth piece click.

Still holding the Emperor's hand. But more now—two more presences joining. The Priestess with her sight. The Empress blazing like something waking up.

*Four who are one. Meeting at last.*

The pressure tripled. The thing in the sky pressing down.

*You were supposed to be prevented*, the wrongness said. *The puppet was supposed to drain her. The vessels fail. You scatter.*

"Supposed to doesn't mean shit."

*Others have tried. Others failed.*

"Because they tried to heal it," the Priestess said. "Close the cracks. That's not possible. The veil isn't sick. It's evolving."

Silence.

"The cracks aren't wounds. They're doors. The veil was supposed to be a membrane. Someone sealed it. That's what's breaking."

The Empress spoke.

"A door that opens both ways."

---

## XV.

She stepped into the circle. The dead grass grew under her feet.

She took the Magician's free hand.

Four points. Four aspects remembering.

She could feel them all. The Emperor's weight. The Priestess's sight. The Magician's will.

And herself. Flooding back.

Not sealing the cracks. Shaping them. Wounds into windows.

The sky stopped cracking.

Through the openings, something looked. Not horror. Curious.

*That's new.*

It withdrew.

The Emperor was on his knees. Not shaking.

"It's quiet. First time ever."

At the edge of the park—the brother. Shadow the size of a building behind him.

*Impressive. Didn't see it coming.*

"It's over."

*The battlefield was never the veil. It was always her.*

---

## XVI.

The Empress didn't run.

"No."

*No what?*

"You don't get me anymore."

*Fifteen years. One night of hand-holding changes that?*

"I don't think anything. I know."

She stepped forward.

"He was my nightmare. He ate my futures. He made me love him. I'm done being food."

*You can't fight me. You're a creator.*

"You're right."

She held out her hand.

"I'm not going to destroy you. I'm going to make something."

Power flowed. From the place buried at eight.

She made a door.

Through it—something the wrongness recognized. Home. Where it came from.

The shadow shuddered. Started flowing toward the door.

*No—*

"You took my choices for fifteen years."

The shadow screamed. Her brother screamed—his own voice.

"Help me. Please. It was inside me—"

She looked at him. The boy who stole her bird. The man who stole her life.

"I know. And I'm sorry."

The door pulled.

The shadow tore away. Her brother collapsed.

Silence.

---

## XVII.

He was still breathing.

The Magician knelt beside his brother. Same face. Nothing behind the eyes.

"Is he dead?"

"Heart's beating."

"Redemption's always available," the Emperor said. "Even for puppets."

"He has to want it."

The Empress didn't touch him.

"I don't know what to do with him."

"Neither do I. We'll figure it out."

"Together," the Priestess said.

"Together," the Emperor said.

"Together."

Three people he'd known less than a day. Three pieces of something.

Above them, the veil hung stable. Membrane instead of wall.

Somewhere beyond, the wrongness slept. Satisfied. Home.

For now.

---

## XVIII.

*The Fool stands at the cliff. Everyone thinks he's falling.*

*He knows he's flying.*

*Four Fools. One step.*

*They fell.*

*No ground.*

*Never was.*

---

**[END CHAPTER ONE]**
