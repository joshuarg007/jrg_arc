# Supporting Characters

## The Grandpa

**Role**: Mentor (deceased). Mr. Brownlow figure—the respectable path, the other way.

### History
Contracted polio as a child. Should never have walked again. Refused this verdict. Jumped into a pool to train his legs when doctors said it was impossible. Walked.

Joined the Army. Served in Korea. There, he found—or was found by—a tradition older than any Western framework for understanding it. A Korean master who saw something in this stubborn American who had already beaten impossible odds.

Third-degree black belt in Taekwondo, earned from a Korean instructor who taught more than combat. The martial discipline was gateway and container for something larger.

### What He Knew
The grandpa's lineage carried comprehensive knowledge:
- **How to perceive the cracks**: Training the eyes and the deeper senses to see the veil's fractures
- **How to survive broken time**: Moving through temporal wrongness without being lost
- **Part of the prophecy**: His tradition held fragments of the four's foretelling

He was preparing the Magician. The physical training was foundation for spiritual capacity. Discipline, respect, honor—these weren't abstractions. They were survival tools for engaging the hidden world.

### Death
Heart attack. He'd had a triple bypass before. Insisted on Marlboros and 7&7s anyway. Survived polio, survived Korea, survived things he never spoke about. Couldn't survive himself.

Preliminary heart attacks. The DTs from the drinking. A body that had been pushed past limits for decades finally presenting the bill.

The Magician was there. Early-to-late teens. Watched a legend end in mundane physical failure.

### After Death
He appeared in dreams. For a while. Teaching continued—or memory of teaching surfaced as dreams. Or something else.

Then he was gone. The dreams stopped.

His disappearance was itself an awakening. The Magician had to stop waiting for guidance. The lineage died with the grandpa. It must be reborn, not inherited. Resurrected through action, not received through instruction.

### Presence in the Story
Dead before the main narrative begins. Present through:
- Memory
- The Magician's training and instincts
- Fragments of teaching that surface at crucial moments
- The absence that forced growth
- Possibly: moments where his influence or will seems to reach beyond death (ambiguous)

## The Bio Dad

**Role**: Fagin figure. The criminal mentor, the chaos domain, the underworld father.

### The Fall
He was training as a priest. Not the Church—something older, stranger. Connected to the wrongness.

He served the other side. Learned things from inside. Then broke. Couldn't sustain the connection. Fell.

What remains: fragments of terrible knowledge. Understanding bought at costs he's still paying. The addiction isn't just physical—it's attempt to drown what he knows.

### Present State
Working addict. Meth, coke, booze, weed—whatever's available. Works hard, spends everything on substances. Functional in the sense that he shows up. Dysfunctional in every other sense.

Violent. Broke a chair against the Magician. The violence isn't controlled—it erupts from pressure he can't manage. The wrongness inside him (not possession, but contamination) expresses as rage.

Lives in the projects. His domain is chaos. The step-mother is also an addict, part of the mess. The Magician was sent here—from the stable home with the rich stepfather to this.

### What He Knows
He knows things from the inside. The wrongness isn't abstract to him—he's touched it, served it, been used by it. He knows how the hunters really work. He knows what the pacts actually cost. He knows the names that shouldn't be spoken.

He won't teach any of this directly. It leaks out in drunken ramblings, paranoid warnings, violent outbursts that contain buried truth. The Magician learned from proximity, not instruction.

### In the Story
The Fagin role: drawing the protagonist into the underworld, providing access to criminal education, modeling darkness as normalcy. But also:
- Source of contaminated knowledge
- Example of what falling looks like
- The violence that opened the Magician's eyes (first real violence as initiation)
- The chaos that forged survival skills

## The Brother

**Role**: Primary antagonist. Outside the four, working to prevent their convergence.

### Nature
Narcissist who believes his own lies. The manipulation is so complete that he doesn't experience himself as manipulative—he's just doing what makes sense, and everyone else is wrong.

Athletic. Beautiful. Charismatic. The favored child. The one who got the mother to choose, who orchestrated the Magician's exile. The party he threw, the blame he deflected—he probably remembers it differently. In his mind, he was the victim somehow.

### The Gift (Unknowing)
He drains. Feeds. Takes energy, attention, life force from others without conscious awareness that he's doing it. People around him deplete. He stays vital.

This is how he found the Empress. The Magician's connection to her drew attention—some resonance the brother sensed. A rich feeding source. He attached.

### The Possession
He is a puppet. Something else is driving.

The wrongness found an ideal vessel: someone already narcissistic, already feeding, already disconnected from empathy. The possession didn't change him much—it amplified, directed, used. He was already empty enough to fill.

He doesn't know he's possessed. Or he knows and has reframed it as power. Or he knows and doesn't care. The lines blur.

### The Seduction
He seduced the Empress through demonic compulsion. The love she feels isn't natural—it was installed. He created a bond that allows continuous feeding.

Her power—the ability to manifest possibilities, to birth futures—flows into him. Through him, to whatever drives him. She can't create because he's eating her creations before they form.

### Function in the Story
- Draining the Empress, weakening one of the four
- The Magician's blood shame and cold necessity
- Proof that some things can't be saved (or: the question of whether even he could be redeemed)
- Instrument of the wrongness's attempt to prevent the convergence
- Will be killed—necessary, clean, without guilt

### The Question
He's a puppet. Something else drives him. He's also a victim.

Does this matter? Redemption is always available—but he has to want it. There's no evidence he does. The Magician's response (cold necessity) may be correct. But the killing of a victim, even a victimized predator, carries weight.

## The Mother

**Role**: The one who chose wrong, then grew.

### Then
She chose the brother. Believed his narratives. Married the rich white stepfather who was indifferent to the Magician at best. When the camping party incident happened, she let the Magician be sent away.

She wasn't evil. She was manipulated, overwhelmed, trying to build stability. But she chose wrong. The damage is real.

### The Distance
Years of separation. The Magician in the projects, in juvie, in the system. She wasn't there.

This isn't a character we excuse. The failure is real. The consequences were severe.

### Now
She grew. Changed. Became, in the Magician's words, "a force to be reckoned with."

How this happened: unknown, to be developed. But the transformation is genuine. She's not the same person who made those choices. She's done the work.

Now she's his mentor. Not a replacement for grandpa—something different. A relationship rebuilt from rubble. The distance became foundation for something neither expected.

### In the Story
- Proof that change is possible
- Complicated reconciliation (not simple forgiveness)
- Contrast with the brother (she changed; he won't)
- Resource for the Magician in the present
- The pattern he's trying not to repeat with his own children (if that element enters the story)

## The Step-Dad

**Role**: Peripheral. The bully who was indifferent.

### Character
Rich. White. Manly in a particular way—not educated, but successful. The kind of man who built something through force of will and doesn't respect what he doesn't understand.

He bullied the Magician. Not with focused cruelty—with indifference that occasionally expressed as contempt. The Magician was the wife's baggage. Not his problem. Not worth investment.

### Function
- The comfortable home the Magician was exiled from
- Symbol of what he never belonged to
- Not the main villain—the brother is. The step-dad just didn't care enough to see.

## The Blood Grandfather

**Role**: The connected one. The legacy of something darker.

### History
The grandmother's ex-husband. Not the martial arts grandpa (who wasn't blood). The actual biological grandfather on that side.

Not mafioso himself, but connected. When he walked into a room, people thought: judge, politician, someone with weight. Charisma and presence that commanded without demanding.

He killed a man once. Released on Make My Day law. The details aren't clear, but the capability was real.

### The Connection
The Magician met him twice:
1. At a wedding—brief, formal
2. On his deathbed—holding him as he died. The Simpsons playing in the background. The surreal mundane of death.

### The Legacy
The grandmother said the Magician looked like him. Or: the aunt said the brother was more like him—the charisma, the presence, the air of authority.

Either way, there's bloodline resonance. The connected grandfather passes something down. Whether it's relevant to the hidden world or just family mythology: to be developed.

### In the Story
- The other masculine legacy (vs. martial arts grandpa)
- Possible connection to criminal underworld orders
- The deathbed moment as formative experience
- Questions about what's inherited vs. chosen

## The Hunters

**Role**: Antagonist force. Servants of the wrongness who believe they're righteous.

### Nature
They hunt those who manifest. Witch-hunters in modern dress. Operating through a church front in Denver—but the church is cover, not cause.

They believe they're purifying. Protecting. Doing God's work. They have no idea they serve the wrongness. Their hunt prevents the convergence of those who could threaten it.

### Methods
- Identification of manifesters through networks, informants, surveillance
- Elimination or capture
- Harvesting—the power can be taken, redirected, consumed
- Some hunters have stolen gifts they use against others

### In the Story
- Direct threat to the four
- The hands and eyes of cosmic prevention
- Irony of believing you fight evil while serving it
- Possible defectors who realize what they actually serve
