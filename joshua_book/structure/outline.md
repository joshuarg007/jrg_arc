# Story Structure

---

## The Opening

**Cards**: Death (XIII), The Hanged Man (XII), The Tower (XVI)

**POV**: Omniscient—external perspective witnessing all four

**Concept**: Four Tower moments happening at the same cosmic instant. Different catastrophes, same synchronous timing. The universe cracks in four places at once.

### The Four Towers (to develop)

Each protagonist experiences their world collapsing. We witness all four in rapid succession or intercut:

**The Magician's Tower**:
- What shatters his world?
- Possibly: learning something about the brother and Empress
- Or: violence that opens his eyes wider
- Or: grandpa's final message/dream ending

**The Emperor's Tower**:
- The containment slipping dramatically
- A moment where he feels the wrongness surge
- Time around him breaking worse than ever

**The Priestess's Tower**:
- A fracture opening that shouldn't exist
- Seeing something through the veil that changes everything
- A secret revealed that demands action

**The Empress's Tower**:
- A crack in the denial
- Moment of clarity about the brother
- Feeling the drain consciously for the first time

### Tone
Destruction. Sacrifice. Upheaval. The Fool's journey begins with everything ending.

## Act Structure (Flexible)

Not rigid three-act. The tarot provides thematic markers, appearing when right.

### Movement One: The Fall / The Fool's Step

**Theme**: Card 0 - The Fool. Stepping off the cliff.

- Opening towers
- Introduction to the four in their separate contexts
- Denver in the 1990s established
- The hidden world's presence felt but not explained
- First meetings (in twos)—hostile or accidental

**Key events**:
- Magician returns to Denver (if he'd left) or emerges from whatever his current situation is
- First temporal wrongness experienced on-page
- Glimpses of the hunters
- The brother's presence established (through Empress)

### Movement Two: The Descent / The Underworld

**Theme**: Moving deeper. Fagin's domain. The projects. Crime. Violence.

- The Magician's underworld education (either flashback or present)
- Emperor's containment strain increasing
- Priestess mapping the fractures
- Empress's power drain worsening
- Pairs meeting, not yet the full four

**Key events**:
- The bio dad's role—exposure to his broken knowledge
- Violence that teaches
- The hunters targeting someone
- Discovery of prophecy fragments
- Someone learns about the brother and Empress connection

### Movement Three: The Recognition

**Theme**: Seeing clearly. The appalling nature of darkness recognized.

- Magician discovers what brother is doing to Empress
- Empress's denial beginning to crack
- Emperor reveals what he carries
- Priestess shares what she's seen in the veil
- Trust building between pairs

**Key events**:
- Confrontation with the brother (not final)
- The manufactured love exposed (or suspected)
- Someone encounters the hunters directly
- The prophecy fragments start connecting
- Question raised: can the four actually converge?

### Movement Four: The Convergence

**Theme**: Coming together. What happens when prophesied meeting occurs.

- All four in proximity
- Effects on Emperor's containment (better or worse?)
- Combined perception of the hidden world
- The wrongness responding—escalation
- The brother's final moves

**Key events**:
- First time all four are present together
- Powers interacting, amplifying, conflicting
- The hunters' assault
- The brother confronted and killed (cold necessity)
- Empress's transformation begins

### Movement Five: The Question

**Theme**: Can the veil be healed? What do they choose?

- The four acting together
- Priestess's attempt at healing (or the decision not to try)
- What the Empress becomes
- What the Emperor's containment means for the group
- Resolution (not necessarily happy, but complete)

**Key events**:
- Engagement with the larger wrongness (not defeating it—it's cosmic)
- Choice that matters more than nature
- The prophecy fulfilled (or subverted)
- Ending that's complete but open to more

## Tone Touchstones

**Grounded cosmic horror**: The incomprehensible experienced by people who still have to eat, sleep, survive.

**The Drizzt principle**: Darkness feels normal until it doesn't. Growing apprehension at what was accepted.

**Not Lovecraft**: No purple prose. No indescribable cop-outs. Horror in the body, not the adjectives.

**Voice**: Street-level. Physical. Action over abstraction. Silence over explanation.

**What we earn**: Any cosmic confrontation must be earned through character investment. We care about the four before we care about the wrongness.

---

## Questions the Story Asks

1. **Can the veil be healed?** (Priestess's hope)
2. **Can the broken be contained?** (Emperor's burden)
3. **Can manufactured love be distinguished from real?** (Empress's violation)
4. **When is killing necessary?** (Magician's cold clarity)
5. **Does being a puppet absolve you?** (The brother's nature)
6. **Does convergence heal or destroy?** (The prophecy's ambiguity)
7. **Can you choose differently than your nature?** (All four)
