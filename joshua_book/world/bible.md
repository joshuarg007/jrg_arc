# World Bible

## The Setting

**Location**: Denver, Colorado
**Era**: 1990s
**Tone**: Grounded urban reality bleeding into cosmic horror

Denver sits a mile above sea level. The air is thinner here. The mountains rise to the west like a wall between worlds. The plains stretch east into nothing. It's a city that feels like a threshold—frontier town grown into metropolis, still carrying that edge-of-something energy.

The 1990s here: Colfax Avenue's motels and pawn shops. The projects off Federal. LoDo before it got cleaned up. Five Points when it still meant something. The mountains always watching.

---

## The Hidden World

There is something behind reality. It has no single name.

Different traditions have called it different things across centuries:
- The Outer
- The Beneath
- The Vast
- Ha-Tehom (the Deep)
- Das Nichts
- The Hum

None of these names are accurate. Language breaks against it. The wrongness doesn't translate.

**What it is**: Not evil in a human sense. Not malevolent. Humans aren't significant enough to hate. It simply IS—vast, indifferent, incomprehensible. A cosmic background radiation of wrongness that predates meaning.

**What it wants**: Unknown. Possibly nothing. Possibly expansion. Possibly it doesn't "want" in any sense we can understand.

---

## The Veil

Reality is fractured.

Between the world we know and the wrongness behind it, there's a membrane—call it the veil. It was never whole, but it was once more intact. Now it's cracked. Fractures run through it like fault lines.

Through these cracks, things leak.

**The primary leak is temporal.** Time doesn't flow correctly near the fractures:
- Lost hours—you leave at noon, arrive at what should be a ten-minute drive, it's dark
- Loops—the same conversation twice, the same stranger passing three times
- Age slippage—children with old eyes, elders who haven't changed in decades
- Déjà vu that's actually memory of a loop you've already lived
- Moments that stretch or compress wrong

The temporal wrongness isn't random. It follows patterns—learnable if you pay attention. But the patterns **change when required**. Just when you think you understand the rules, they shift. As if something is watching you learn.

**Can the veil be healed?**

This is the central question. The High Priestess believes it can. Others say she's naive—the best we can do is manage the cracks, hold the line. The truth is unknown. The story asks this question; it doesn't assume the answer.

---

## Perception

Not everyone can see the hidden world.

**Who sees:**
- "Those worthy" (an old phrase from multiple traditions—meaning unclear, possibly circular)
- First real violence often opens the eyes—blood as initiation
- Certain bloodlines carry the sight
- Proximity to the cracks can force perception
- Some are trained to see; some are broken into seeing

**What seeing costs:**
Once you perceive the wrongness, you can't unpercieve it. The cracks are always there now, in peripheral vision. Time's stutters become noticeable. You feel the veil's fragility.

Most who see don't last long. They break, or they're broken, or they make pacts to make it stop.

---

## Manifestation

Some people can make things real.

**The power**: Thought, fear, desire, will—externalized. What's inside becomes outside. Like the film *Sphere*—the boundary between imagination and reality becomes permeable.

**Two modes:**
1. **Untrained**: Fear creates. Nightmares manifest. Anxiety births its objects. Dangerous, uncontrolled, often lethal to the manifester or those nearby.

2. **Trained**: Will creates. Focused discipline allows intentional manifestation. The Hermetic traditions call this "as above, so below"—the mental becoming physical through structured practice.

**The hunters**: Those who manifest are hunted. Not by ordinary people—by evil-adjacent forces. Groups serving the wrongness itself, though they may not know that's what they serve. They call it purification, or protection, or God's work. They hunt witches.

Their goal isn't just elimination. They harvest. They steal. The power can be transferred, consumed, redirected.

---

## Possession and Pacts

The wrongness can get inside people.

**How it happens:**
- Pacts—conscious or unconscious deals. Sometimes you know what you're agreeing to. Sometimes you just wanted the pain to stop, and something answered.
- Invitation through behavior—certain patterns of darkness create openings
- Bloodline vulnerability—some families are susceptible
- Proximity to fractures—spending too long near the cracks

**What it looks like:**
The possessed aren't Hollywood demonic. They're functional. They go to work. They have relationships. But something else is driving—using them as instruments, feeding through them, seeing through their eyes.

The puppet may not know. The puppet may know and not care. The puppet may be screaming inside.

**Redemption**: Always available. This is a cosmic law that even the wrongness can't override. Any possessed being can be saved—if they want it, if someone can reach them, if the timing aligns. But the window can close. And sometimes the puppet has become the demon has become the puppet, and there's nothing left to save.

---

## The Orders

Multiple traditions have developed to understand and survive the hidden world. They don't agree. They don't coordinate. Some actively oppose each other.

**Western Esoteric**: Hermetic orders, Rosicrucian lineages, ceremonial magic traditions. Systematic, hierarchical, focused on knowledge and control. They believe understanding is power.

**Eastern Traditions**: Martial lineages that are more than martial. Korean, Chinese, Japanese, Indian schools where physical discipline opens spiritual perception. The body as gateway.

**Criminal Underworld Orders**: Old families, cartels, syndicates that have been around long enough to brush against the hidden world. They don't use mystical language—they talk about luck, about protection, about debts that can't be paid in money. But they know.

**The Fallen**: Those who served the wrongness and broke. Scattered individuals carrying fragments of terrible knowledge. Not organized. Often addicts, often broken, often dangerous. They know things from inside.

**The Hunters**: Those who serve the wrongness while believing they oppose it. Witch-hunters, purifiers, fanatics. They think they're righteous. They're instruments.

---

## The Prophecy

Multiple traditions, across cultures and centuries, have foreseen a convergence.

Four figures. Four aspects of something. Meeting at a particular moment.

No single tradition has the complete picture. The Hermetic orders have fragments. The Korean martial lineages carry pieces passed down through generations. The old criminal families whisper about a reckoning. Even the wrongness itself seems to have anticipated this—and moved to prevent it.

**What the prophecy says (compiled from fragments):**
- Four who are one, broken into four
- When the cracks reach critical, they will meet
- Their meeting is either healing or ending
- Someone will try to stop them
- Someone will try to use them
- What they choose matters more than what they are

**Who knows:**
- The grandpa figure knew. His tradition carried part of it.
- The wrongness knows—and sends its instruments against them
- Some of the orders know and have chosen sides
- The four themselves don't know. Not yet.

## Denver Specifics

**The Cracks**: Denver has more than its share. The altitude, the geology, the convergence of ley lines or whatever you want to call them—the veil is thin here.

Specific locations where time runs wrong:
- Certain rooms in the Brown Palace
- A stretch of Colfax near the Bluebird
- The basement of a building in Five Points
- Cheesman Park after midnight (built on a graveyard, poorly moved)
- A particular alley in the projects off Federal

**The Players**: Denver's hidden world in the 1990s includes:
- What's left of a Hermetic lodge, mostly old money pretending
- A Korean martial arts school that's more than it appears
- Street-level practitioners who learned the hard way
- A family in the criminal underworld that's been here since mining days
- The hunters, operating through a church front
- The fallen priest in the projects, who knows too much

## Tone and Style Notes

**Not Lovecraft's style:**
- No purple prose, no adjective avalanches
- No "indescribable" cop-outs—describe it or don't mention it
- No fainting protagonists—these are people who can't afford to break
- No racist undertones or "degenerate" bloodline nonsense
- The horror is felt in the body, not narrated in superlatives

**What we're doing instead:**
- Street-level perspective on cosmic scale
- Survival-mode consciousness—characters who function because they have to
- Physical, somatic horror—nausea, pressure, wrongness in the gut
- The incomprehensible experienced by people who still need to make rent
- Silence and implication over explanation
- The human mattering precisely because the cosmos doesn't care

**The Drizzt principle**: The wrongness should feel natural from inside—like there's nothing else, this is just how it is. But underneath, a growing sense of being appalled. Characters who were raised in darkness recognizing, slowly, that it's darkness.
